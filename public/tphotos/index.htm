SPEECHES

by

Paul R. Baier

The Louisiana Bar Foundation proudly announces the publication of LSU Law Professor Paul R. Baier's book, SPEECHES, with a foreword by Justice Harry A. Blackmun. The book tells the story of Eldon Fallon's invitation to Professor Baier to become the first Scholar-in-Residence of the LBF. Thereafter, the reader listens anew to our first Scholar-in-Residence's oration unveiling the Rosenthal Portrait of Chief Justice Edward Douglas White and to his speech "Time and the Court," on the occasion of the Bicentennial of the Supreme Court of the United States. Vibrant verbal portraits of Chief Justice William H. Rehnquist, Justice Lewis F. Powell, Jr., Judge Albert Tate, Jr., and other jurists fill the book. Stage appearances at the Library of Congress and the Social Law Library, Boston, add teasing drama to the book. Of these speeches, Justice Blackmun writes "They range from intense verbal portraits of jurists the author has admired-with fine and peppery doses of Holmes and Scalia-to serious detailed reviews of the work of the Supreme Court at critical points." Judge Eldon Fallon adds: "Professor Baier knows his history. He has a way with words. His speeches, like Holmes's before him, touch hearts."

 O*NET™ Companion to Occupational Outlook Handbook with detail Summaries-18.1

The 18.1 database release of O*NET™ brings the total of comprehensively updated occupations to 921. This book matches O*NET detailed data and descriptions with the Occupational Outlook Handbook (OOH) profiles that includes the overwhelming majority of jobs in the United States. Over 720 detailed data-level occupations from O*NET provide an enormous depth of information on worker attributes and job characteristics encompassed in the OOH occupation profiles. The data has been organized and ranked by importance and relevance for all occupations within the context of the content model used by O*NET.
In addition to the O*NET database, information from supplemental databases and crosswalks have been included in the listings in this book:
• The Tools and Technology (T2) database provides information for each occupation on machines, equipment, tools, and software that workers may use for optimal functioning in a high performance workplace. The current T2 database identifies over 53,000 tools and technologies for 774 occupations.

• The crosswalk to the Classification of Instructional Programs (CIP) provides listings of fields of study and programs appropriate to each occupation.

• The crosswalk to the Registered Apprenticeship Partners Information Data System (RAPIDS) provides listings from over 24,000 apprenticeship programs appropriate to each occupation.

Louisiana Mineral Law Treatise
edited by Patrick H. Martin

The Louisiana Mineral Law Treatise provides readers, primarily attorneys and landmen, a compact and convenient guide to many of the issues and topics that they frequently face on oil and gas ownership, leasing and development in Louisiana. In the four decades that have passed since the drafting and enactment of the Louisiana Mineral Code numerous cases have arisen and have been decided by the courts under the articles of this Code. Not so numerous but still significant have been the legislative changes to the Mineral Code. Several topics of common interest are not directly addressed by the Mineral Code. This Treatise brings together in-depth treatment of the oil and gas law that has arisen under the Mineral Code and a general introduction to several related topics, including the leasing of state and OCS lands, the practice of Conservation law and water law.
